Worlds

Worlds is the biggest tournament in LoL Esports. It is the tournament that all pro players seek to prove themselves as the best in the world.
Out of the 12 regions listed above, Korea has been the most dominant, with China only gaining Worlds trophies in 2018, 2019 and 2021.

History

Each year since 2011, a World Champion has been crowned. The first season is the only season where EU was able to become World Champion, with Fnatic as the first to win the tournament.

In 2012 (Season 2), more regions were added to the tournament, and this was the first time Korea set foot on the World Stage. This was also the year where the LMS (now PCS) hoisted the first ever Summoner's Cup, the most coveted trophy in LoL Esports.

In 2013, the the best player in the game's history made their debut on the World stage. Faker was 17 when he first won Worlds 2013 (Season 3) with SKT T1K.

In 2014, Samsung White (SSW) became World Champions, and this was the only year where the Support was named MVP. This was also the year where Imagine Dragons performed Warriors.


In 2015, the first League of Legends dynasty was born, with SKT T1 winning their second World Championship. This is the most dominant win in Worlds history, with SKT only dropping 1 game in the finals, never losing a single game in the entire tournament up until that point.


In 2016, the SKT dynasty continues with their win in MSI 2016 and Worlds 2016. This was the year Faker was finally crowned MVP. This would be Faker's 3rd World Championship title.


In 2017, Samsung Galaxy, the 2016 2nd placer, defeated SKT in a 3-0 sweep. This would be the end of the SKT dynasty. This would also mark a new era in LoL Esports.


2018 would be the first time a team from the LPL would lift the Summoner's Cup with Invictus Gaming defeating Fnatic in a 3-0 sweep.


In 2019, G2 Esports, winner of MSI 2019 (the 2nd EU International trophy ever), defeated SKT in the semifinals, to be the second EU team to make it to the World Finals, only to lose to China's FunPlus Phoenix 3-0.


In 2020, MSI was cancelled, but Worlds still occured. This would be the First time in 3 years that a team from the LCK would make it to the World Finals - Daniel Drakos.
Damwon Gaming (DWG, now Damwon Gaming Kia (DK)). DWG went on to win the Finals versus Suning Gaming.


With Worlds 2021 having recently closed, Edward Gaming (EDG) defeat DK in an intense 5-game series, when everyone predicted DK to gain their 2nd title. This is the most historic year with the most best of 5s going to 5 games.

Due to the impact of COVID, Riot had to make a sudden shift in venue for Worlds 2021, moving the entire tournament from China, to a studio in Reykjavik, Iceland.

"We were fortunate to be able to utilize these relationships [with the Icelandic authorities]. Also, some operational planning from our recent events in Iceland, which helped us expedite our planning, expedite the sourcing of venues, hotels and vendors under the short timelines." - Jarret Siegel
